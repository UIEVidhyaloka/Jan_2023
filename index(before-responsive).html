Digital School

The COVID- 19 pandemic has shown us how challenging it is for children in rural and semi-urban areas to access quality education, whether online or offline. As a consequence, there are a large number of children in danger of being pushed out of formal education, and the marginalized populations, as always, remain at greater risk. The Digital School Program was designed to ensure no child is deprived of learning.This program aims to provide multimedia content aligned with the school curriculum in a ready-to-consume format so children from even the remotest villages in India can access quality learning content just like their urban counterparts.

Learn Through Television

Though India is towards digitalisation era many of the households in the remote villages do not have smart phones. The lockdown impacted the students in rural India, with limited access to learning opportunities.

Therefore to reach more students eVidyaloka came up with the initiative of teaching through Television, as television is one of the most easily available commodities in the all social strata.

Keeping this in mind eVidyaloka launched mass media learning i.e. Teach through Television in October 2020.

Goals

  • To utilize the existing infrastructure available within communities to cater to the learning needs of the children

  • Enable learning for students who are devoid of smartphones

  • Lift crores of children by giving them the education and skills to be able to part icipate in the economy

eVidyaloka, in partnership with local cable 1V network operators, broadcast video lessons on cable 1V in the respective regional language for state board curriculum in Maths and Science.To know more on the schedule, visit - https://www.evidyaloka.org/tttinfo/
