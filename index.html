The Digital School Program was designed to ensure no child is deprived of learning. The COVID- 19 pandemic has shown us how challenging it is for children in rural and semi-urban areas to access quality education, whether online or offline. As a consequence, there are a large number of children in danger of being pushed out of formal education, and the marginalized populations, as always, remain at greater risk.

This program aims to provide multimedia content aligned with the school curriculum in a ready-to-consume format so children from even the remotest villages in India can access quality learning content just like their urban counterparts.

Research indicates that images go straight from the eyes to long-term memory, while text takes a more circuitous route first to short-term memory and then to long-term memory.

We realized that the grade level appropriate videos offer numerous benefits when it comes to learning. The videos only helped students to visualize abstract or complex subjects more easily than text which aided the understanding of concepts and ideas.

Leadership Program

Leadership Program is a three-month program that aims to groom students‚ÄØ who are inquisitive about learning‚ÄØmore.‚ÄØStudents set small targets based on the SMART goals framework and work towards achieving them with guidance from a mentor. It is concluded with the students presenting in the form of a TED-style talk.

It was first introduced as a pilot program in 2017-18 in two states, Jharkhand & Karnataka. The results were tremendous.The program has now been scaled to include more states.

In AY 2021-22, LC 2.0 was introduced, a bigger, better and bolder version of the original program. The schools were closed and classrooms with digital infrastructure were inaccessible.

Inspite of these challenges, we had 105 students participating from 8 states. We had over 30 mentors who groomed these students to achieve their goals.

The mentors stayed connected to their students via the available modes of communication, right from video calls to basic phone calls to SMS, through the months of September 2021 to January 2022. The TED style talks by the students were recorded in their locations and presented for evaluation.

V-Gnyana

V-Gnyana is the flagship quizzing event of eVidyaloka co-created with LTI. Launched in 2020, the event is a dedicated quiz to foster competitive quizzing among the students of rural India. In AY, V-Gnyana had 2,475 Students from 140 schools across 7 states participating in teams of 3.

The top-performing 95 teams from all 7 states qualified for the semi-finals.

With the first 3 rounds being pen and paper based and the last two being online, the national level quiz was a nail biting event for the viewers. V-Gnyana is another proof that, given the required infrastructure and access to information, children of rural India are on par with their urban counterparts.

National Student Innovation Challenge

National Student Innovation Challenge (NSIC) nurtures talent and leadership for Grade 6, 7 & 8 students. It is a unique PAN-India contest powered by LTI and conducted by eVidyaloka. NSIC was started 5 years ago to celebrate the rural and urban spirit. This is done by pairing children from rural India with their urban counterparts under a volunteer mentor. Together, they work on a project that helps address one of the UN's Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

For NSIC Season 5, 130 registrations were received from across the states of India. Topics chosen by students are based on eradication of poverty, good health & well being, clean water & sanitation, and responsible consumption & production. Phase 2 is the final round where the finalist teams were further regrouped into 21 teams based on common problem statements. Based on the evaluation from LTI Jury panels 10 teams emerged as winners for the National Students Innovation Challenge, Season 5.
